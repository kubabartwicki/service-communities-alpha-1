Why you should set up a service community

Posted by: Kuba Bartwicki, Posted on: 14 March 2019

Service communities are networks of people from across the public sector who work together to design and deliver an end-to-end service, like start a business or get health benefits.

Anyone who works on a service can be a part of a community, whether they work in policy, delivery, or operations. Our team is working to enable anyone to set up a community so that we can grow this approach and improve end-to-end services in government.

Make your service better for users

Since the first one started in 2017, service communities helped people join up government services by working together across boundaries. Community members managed to improve the navigation on GOV.UK by publishing step-by-step guides to their services, like Set up a limited company. They also managed to reduce duplication in government by reusing resources from one another. Read more about what service communities have accomplished.

Shape direction of your service

Running a service community makes it easier for you to influence direction of your service. Having an awareness of what everybody in government is doing on a given end-to-end service makes it easier to spot opportunities for improvement. Recently, a member of the Start a business community used another department’s channel to communicate with their users, instead of having to build a new one.

Improve your facilitation and collaboration skills

Once your community is up and running, you will have to design and lead workshops, keep track of what the community is working on, and onboard new people. Our team can help you with this and give you the tools and support you need to be a great facilitator.

Connect with others who work on your service

Setting up a community will enable you to get a full overview of who does what on the wider context of your service. This can have numerous benefits, including increased awareness of research activities conducted across government. Having this means you can learn from each other’s successes and failures or ask for help when you need it.

Get started

Services come in all shapes and sizes, and so do service communities. For example, the import-export community includes 90 people from over 10 organisations, but the health benefits community includes 10 people from 2 organisations. If you are not sure what the wider context of your service is yet, don’t worry – our team can help. There is also guidance on the Service Manual.

Our team can help with scoping, but also designing workshops. We provide guidance on how to collaborate in a sustainable way and case studies showing how others collaborated.

If you want to set up a service community for your end-to-end service, email us or read more about working across organisational boundaries.
