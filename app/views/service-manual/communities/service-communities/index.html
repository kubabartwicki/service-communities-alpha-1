  • Set up a service community

A service community is a network of people from across the public sector who work together to design and deliver an end-to-end service. By end-to-end services, we mean wider user journeys from the point where the user starts trying to achieve a goal to the point when they’re finished.

You can use service communities if you need to learn what other teams are doing on the wider user journey your service is a part of, develop a common understanding of your users and their needs, or address common collaboration challenges such as different ways of working and maintaining momentum.

You will get:

  • a network of people who work on your service
  • guidance on how to collaborate in a sustainable way
  • case studies showing how others collaborated
  • a forum to get answers on your day-to-day work
  • support in publishing the outputs of your work in the form of case studies
  • support in improving the visibility of your service on GOV.UK

A service community meets regularly and is open to people from all roles, including policy, delivery and operations. There are 4 active service communities:

  • Start a business
  • Employ someone
  • Import-export
  • Get health benefits

To join a service community or learn more, email the service communities team at GDS or join the #servicecommunities channel on the cross-government Slack.

Set up a service community

If the service you’re working on doesn’t have a service community, you might want to set one up. Anyone can set up and run a service community. It’s a great chance to develop your leadership skills and help shape the direction of the service you work on. Learn more about why you should set up a service community.
