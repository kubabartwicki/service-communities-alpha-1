Service communities are networks of people with different skills that work across departments and non-government organisations to design and deliver end-to-end services that work better for users.

By end-to-end services, we mean wider user journeys from the point where the user starts trying to achieve a goal to the point when theyâ€™re finished.

Service communities can help you:

  • learn what other teams are doing on the wider user journey your service is a part of
  • develop a common understanding of your users and their needs
  • address common collaboration challenges such as different ways of working and maintaining momentum

Service communities meet regularly and are open to people from all roles, including policy, delivery and operations. There are 4 active service communities:

  • Start a business
  • Employ someone
  • Import-Export
  • Get health benefits

To join a service community or learn more, email the service communities team at GDS or join the #servicecommunities channel on the cross-government Slack.

Run a service community

Anyone can start and run a service community. The service communities team can help you get the scope right and provide resources on how to run it. Learn more about how to run a service community.
