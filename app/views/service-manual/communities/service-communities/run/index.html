What service communities are

Service communities enable cross-government collaboration by making it easier for people to work together on an end-to-end service.

You might be interested in service communities if you think your work could benefit from talking to teams across government that are working on a similar service.

They are open to people in all roles.

Before you start a community

  • Create a proposal document. This will help you articulate why you need a service community
  • Think about who needs to be involved. Include people from policy, operations and digital, and anyone else that helps shapes the service

In your first few workshops

  • Create a service ladnscape. This will help you get a common understanding of the problem space and the scope of your community
  • Agree on a terms of refrence. This articulates your community objectives and ways of working
  • Set up communcation channels, so that you can stay in contact with your community. Make sure these channels are inclusive and accessible to everyone

Deliver against your objectives

  • Develop a common language in your community, so that you're all referring to the same thing
  • Create a step-step-step to help simplify the user jouney on GOV.UK
  • Track your progress against your obejctives, so that you know you're developing your community in the right way
